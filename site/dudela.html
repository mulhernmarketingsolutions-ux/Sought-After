The One Thing

Most parenting brands lecture. Dudela needed to joke around instead.

Dudela isn't just a podcast — it's a whole small world for expecting and new dads: a prep kit, a free guide, and a show, built around one honest idea, that most dad content either lectures you or ignores you completely. The brand had to sound like a friend who's already been through it, not another authority telling you what to do. So the identity leaned all the way into that: a name and mark with some personality, a voice built on plain talk and inside jokes, and a site structure that gets a nervous new dad to something useful fast instead of burying it in soft parenting-blog copy.

That's the whole difference between Dudela and everything else in the category — it doesn't perform empathy. It just talks like a guy who gets it.

Merch dads actually want to wear — not just a logo slapped on a hat.

The podcast setup — real conversations, no scripts, no lectures.

We started with an idea and a bunch of notes. They helped us figure out who we were actually building this for and turned it into a brand that feels real. Now when people land on our site, they get it.
